In an obsidian, inky oblivion, a gilded hero was born. Rapacious and ravenous, yet resplendently rotund, his luminescent presence evoked a hopeful contrast to his perilous plight. Doomed to eternally dance in the labyrinthian night lands diligently guarded by vengeful spirits, his only hope of survival was to extinguish the sparks of illumination, hidden throughout the realm and drawing on his innate luminescence. Racing, rolling, and running he did all he could to desperately douse each spark before his mortal foes could close in and drain him of his last vestiges of power, without which he would be cursed to join the shadows, another peripatetic soldier in the ghostly garrison. His fervent, frenetic, ferocious zeal to avoid such a fate continuously infuriated his adversaries as he eluded capture and progressed in his quest. He could not say how long he was pursued, nor how many sparks he destroyed, but in the end our golden protagonist was not to be denied. Upon destroying the last leech on his power, he unveiled his full luminance to briefly banish the tireless wraiths, before they could, once again, enact ensorcellment and enchantment to scatter and dilute his intrinsic potency.

This is the backstory I contrived in the naivete of childhood, one that I shamelessly used to fill in underwhelming, uninspiring explanations for phenomena. What sounds more interesting to you?
